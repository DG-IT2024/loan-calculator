This uses flat-rate interest: rate × principal × term, spread evenly across all months. Interest does not decline as you pay down principal — this matches how the coop's ledger actually calculates charges (confirmed against a real subsidiary ledger, where monthly interest stayed constant even as the balance dropped). This is not the same as a diminishing-balance/amortizing loan, and the effective annual rate is higher than the stated flat rate implies. The schedule below reflects this: principal is repaid in equal installments and "Balance" is the remaining principal only — it does not represent an amortizing payoff amount, since interest here doesn't compound or recalculate on balance.
